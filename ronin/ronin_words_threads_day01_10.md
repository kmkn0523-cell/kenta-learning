## DAY 01（月）｜一期一会
**フォーマット：1（画像型）／フック：型E（共感フック）**

### 朝投稿（8:00 AM EST）画像あり
```
You've been in this conversation before.

Or have you?

一期一会 — Ichi-go Ichi-e

"This moment will never come again."

Every word you say today
is being spoken for the first and last time.

This meal. This laugh. This quiet moment.

Gone the second it ends.

Are you actually here for it?

#JapaneseWisdom #Zen #Mindfulness
```

## DAY 04（木）｜背水の陣
**フォーマット：1（画像型）／フック：型C（数字・事実フック）**

### 朝投稿（8:00 AM EST）画像あり
```
2,200 years ago, a general burned his own boats
after crossing a river.

Japan called this strategy:

背水の陣 — Haisui no jin

"Fight with your back to the river."

No retreat. No backup plan. No safety net.

His soldiers had one option left.

Win.

What's the "backup plan" that's been
quietly killing your main plan?

#JapaneseWisdom #Bushido #Mindset
```

### 夜投稿（8:00 PM EST）テキストのみ
```
Comfort is the enemy of commitment.

The samurai removed the option to retreat.

So he had no choice but to win.
```

## DAY 05（金）｜諸行無常
**フォーマット：4（ストーリー型）／フック：型F（衝撃事実フック）**

### 朝投稿（8:00 AM EST）画像あり
```
1,500 years ago, Buddhist monks
taught Japan something most people
still haven't accepted.

They called it:

諸行無常 — Shogyō mujō

"Nothing in this world stays the same."

Not your pain.
Not your joy.
Not the version of you reading this.

The cherry blossom is beautiful
because it falls.

The samurai lived fully
because he accepted he would die.

Impermanence isn't the problem.

It's the whole point.

#JapaneseWisdom #Zen #Stoicism
```

### 夜投稿（8:00 PM EST）テキストのみ
```
The hardest thing to accept:

This too shall pass —

including the good parts.
```

## DAY 06（土）｜石の上にも三年
**フォーマット：1（画像型）／フック：型A（逆説フック）**

### 朝投稿（8:00 AM EST）画像あり
```
The samurai didn't believe in 90-day results.

He believed in this:

石の上にも三年 — Ishi no ue ni mo san nen

"Even a cold stone warms
after three years of sitting on it."

Modern culture says: quit if it's not working.

The samurai sat on cold stones for years.

One of them built a civilization
that lasted 700 years.

The other builds apps.

What have you quit before the stone had time to warm?

#JapaneseWisdom #Patience #Bushido
```

### 夜投稿（8:00 PM EST）テキストのみ
```
You're not stuck.

You're still warming the stone.

Three years feels long —
until you're three years in.
```

## DAY 07（日）｜虎穴に入らずんば虎子を得ず
**フォーマット：2（テキスト型）／フック：型E（共感フック）**

### 朝投稿（8:00 AM EST）テキストのみ
```
There's something you've been
circling for months. Maybe years.

虎穴に入らずんば虎子を得ず
Koketsu ni irazunba koji o ezu

"You cannot get the tiger's cub
without entering the tiger's den."

The thing you want most isn't hidden.

It's guarded.

By difficulty. By embarrassment.
By the fear that makes most people turn back.

That fear is the filter.

You're supposed to feel it.
Walk through anyway.
```

### 夜投稿（8:00 PM EST）テキストのみ
```
Fear guards everything worth having.

That's not a warning.

That's a map.
```

## DAY 08（月）｜覚悟を決めた者は強い
**フォーマット：1（画像型）／フック：型D（沈黙フック）**

### 朝投稿（8:00 AM EST）画像あり
```
覚悟

Most people don't know this word.

覚悟を決めた者は強い — Kakugo o kimeta mono wa tsuyoi

"One who has truly decided is unbreakable."

Not the strongest. Not the smartest.

The most decided.

There's a difference between:

"I'd like to..."
"Maybe I'll..."
"I will."

Those three words live in different universes.

Which one are you living in?

#JapaneseWisdom #Mindset #Bushido
```

```
The moment you truly decide —
not hope, not wish —

decide.

Something shifts.
```
