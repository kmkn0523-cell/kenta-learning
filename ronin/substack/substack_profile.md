### About RoninWords

A ronin had no master. No lord to follow, no prescribed path to walk.

He had only his craft — and the question every free person eventually faces: *What do I live for, now that I must decide for myself?*

**RoninWords** is a weekly publication for people asking that same question.

Every week, I go deep on a single concept from Japanese philosophy — Ikigai, Wabi-Sabi, Mushin, Shokunin, Ma, Hara Hachi Bu — not as a self-help framework, but as a way of seeing. Each essay traces the cultural roots of the concept, corrects what the West got wrong, and delivers something you can actually use: a question to sit with, a practice to try, a way of being you hadn't considered.

The writing is direct. The ideas are ancient. The application is today.

**What you get as a paid subscriber:**

- One long-form essay per week (2,000–2,500 words)
- Cultural context you won't find in a TED talk or productivity blog
- Practical journaling prompts and reflection exercises
- Access to the full archive

**Free subscribers** receive occasional essays and updates.

This is not a newsletter about Japan. It's a newsletter about living well — using Japan's oldest ideas as the lens.

*If you've ever felt that Western self-help misses something essential, you're in the right place.*

I write about Japanese philosophy for people who are done with easy answers.

RoninWords began as a daily practice — one proverb, one concept, one question per day. It grew into something I couldn't stop doing. Not because it made me productive. Because it made me honest.
