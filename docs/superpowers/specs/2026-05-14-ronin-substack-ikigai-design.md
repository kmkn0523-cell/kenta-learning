**Status:** Approved

## Overview

A long-form Substack article (2,000〜2,500 words) for @RoninWords, targeting English-speaking Western men aged 25–45 interested in self-improvement and Eastern philosophy. The article is paid content and uses a contrarian hook to reframe the popular Western "Ikigai diagram" misinterpretation, then delivers the authentic Japanese meaning and practical self-discovery questions.

---

## Target Audience

- Western men, 25–45 years old
- Already familiar with the word "Ikigai" (likely from the 4-circle diagram)
- Interested in self-improvement, minimalism, Eastern philosophy
- Feeling pressure to find "one perfect life purpose"

## Article Structure

### 1. Hook (200 words)
Open with a provocative claim: the Ikigai diagram readers saw on Instagram was not created by a Japanese person. Use this to create a "wait, I didn't know that" moment. Establish credibility immediately.

### 2. The Western Distortion (400 words)
- Trace the origin of the 4-circle Venn diagram (created by Marc Winn in 2014, not Japanese)
- Contrast with the original Japanese concept: etymology of 生き甲斐 (iki = life/living + gai = worth/value)
- Show how the Western version turned a daily-life philosophy into an overwhelming life-mission quest

### 3. What Ikigai Actually Means (400 words)
- The authentic meaning: small, daily reasons to get up in the morning
- Reference Okinawa's longevity culture (Blue Zone research)
- Ikigai is not one grand answer — it's plural, humble, present-tense

### 4. Why This Matters for You (400 words)
- Dismantle the Western pressure: "you must find your one true calling"
- Ikigai can be your morning coffee, your craft, your child's laugh
- It's allowed to change. It's allowed to be small.
- Reframe: freedom from the tyranny of purpose-seeking

### 5. The 7 Questions (400 words)
A journaling exercise to excavate personal Ikigai. Questions include:
1. What did you do today that made time disappear?
2. What would you do even if no one paid you or praised you?
3. What small moment this week made you feel alive?
4. What skill do you have that comes naturally but feels effortful to others?
5. What do people thank you for, without you asking?
6. What would you regret not doing at the end of your life?
7. What would the 80-year-old version of you say to you right now?

### 6. Closing (150 words)
- Bring it back to the Samurai/Ronin brand voice
- Reinforce: Ikigai is not found — it is noticed
- CTA: subscribe, share, journal prompt for replies
